Helping you find work with a future

Finding work that will continue to be in demand in a world of rapid techonological change can be a challenge. This site will make employment and training decisions a bit easier by showing which industries and job skills are in demand now, how much they pay, what skills and training you will need and where and how you can maximise your chances of scoring long lasting, satisfying employment.

Future proof your worklife

CareerChoices uses data science to find and highlight current and future opportunites for work using data drawn from the Australian Taxation Office, the Department of Education and Training, the Department of Industry, the Australian Bureau of Statistics and the Department of Employment.

Dive in and start your journey to a bright new future

Benefits

How can you benefit from digging deeper?

Following the money

Drawing on data from the migrant employment data, CareerChoices can show you the areas that are in current demand - jobs that employers can't find enough local candidates to fill.

Jobs for the future

Using the Bureau of Statistics experimental industry data you can identify jobs that are only just being invented and get in on the ground floor

Train Smarter

Look at completion and drop out rates for various courses to inform your training decsions, then look for or create training opportunities that will maximise your chances of future success.

Datasets Used

Dataset Name: 
Higher Education Attrition Rates 2005-2013
Publishing Organisation/Agency: 
Department of Education and Training
Jurisdiction of Data: 
Australian Government
Dataset URL: 
https://data.gov.au/dataset/higher-education-attrition-rates-2005-2013
How the data was used: 
Once a site visitor has identified an area of study that might provide future employment opportunities they can view attrition rates for their area of study, giving them an idea of how difficult it might be to complete a course of study in that area, or which universities or training organisations might best fulfil their training aspirations.
